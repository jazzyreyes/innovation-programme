Module 01

Session format

Every session runs the same four-part shape so it becomes muscle memory. Roughly half on education and capturing learning, half on applying it to real workflows. The first two sessions are more setup-focused.

0:00–0:15
Part 1 · 15 min

Wins & learnings

Each person shares one thing they tried since last session and what they learned. This is the accountability moment, homework made visible. Bryton facilitates, connecting patterns across business units.

0:15–0:35
Part 2 · 20 min

Teach

One concept, framework, or skill per session, drawn from The AI-Driven Leader and Ideal Flow. Ends with how it applies to the business specifically. Bryton or a team member teaches what they are learning.

0:35–1:15
Part 3 · 40 min

Work + market & tool scan

The working block where SOPs get captured and workflows redesigned, rotating one person's workflow under the microscope each session. A short market and tool scan folds in here: surface new tools and AI capabilities and decide what to evaluate.

1:15–1:30
Part 4 · 15 min

Commit & close

Each person commits to one experiment or SOP draft before next session, written down and logged in the tracker. Each attendee can also name one opportunity to raise the business, captured as a commitment.

Module 02

Curriculum arc

The teach sequence builds confidence as it goes: see your work, choose what to transform, do it well, do it safely, then apply it to the firm's most important work. Roughly 9–10 sessions across about 20 weeks at bi-weekly cadence, through end of Q3.

1

Workflow archaeology

2–3 sessions · opener

See your own work as a system before transforming it.

Core skill
Mapping a current-state workflow honestly, including the "shadow work" nobody documents
Why it fits
You can't AI-enable what you can't see; skipping this automates chaos
Output
Every member maps one workflow they own with brutal honesty
2

Identifying high-leverage workflows

1–2 sessions
Core skill
A scoring framework: frequency, time spent, error rate, AI-suitability, business impact
Why it fits
Serves the 90-day goal of shipping real transformations; filters the noise
Output
A ranked transformation backlog the team owns together
3

Prompting as a professional skill

2 sessions
Core skill
Structured prompting, context engineering, prompt libraries, iteration loops
Why it fits
Most teams plateau at basic prompts and never get the real value
Output
Each person builds a personal prompt library of 5–10 prompts they use weekly
4

The trust stack

1 session / recurring opener
Core skill
Verification habits; knowing AI's failure modes; building review checkpoints
Why it fits
A regulated industry has compliance stakes; build the muscle early
Output
A shared "trust ladder" for when AI output ships vs needs human review
5

AI-augmented client conversations

3 sessions · advisors co-lead
Core skill
Using AI for prep, summarization, scenario modeling, follow-up, personalization at scale
Why it fits
Ties to Advisor Desk and the firm's strategic direction; real, not theoretical
Output
2–3 advisor workflows redesigned with AI augmentation
Recommended sequence: Workflow archaeology → high-leverage workflows → prompting → trust stack → AI-augmented client conversations. You move from seeing the work, to choosing what to transform, to doing it well, to doing it safely, to doing it in the highest-stakes context. Each phase compounds the last.
Module 03

Session 1 plan

Kickoff + workflow archaeology, part 1. 90 minutes, in person if possible. Open with stakes and an invitation, not content.

Title

Duration
90 minutes
Location

Pre-work, sent a week ahead

Watch a short workflow-mapping video, and come prepared to talk about one workflow you own that you find frustrating, repetitive, or time-consuming. Don't document it yet, just have it in your head.

Agenda

0:00–0:10
10 min

Welcome & why

Why this strike team exists, what success looks like at 90 days and 12 months, why these people were chosen, and the bar: everyone has Claude, everyone experiments, everyone commits. Tone: invitation, not assignment.

0:10–0:20
10 min

How we work

Walk through the cadence, the four-part structure, the Loop workspace on screen, and the non-negotiables: homework gets done, experiments tracked, commitments visible.

0:20–0:45
25 min · teach

Workflow archaeology

You can't transform what you can't see. Shadow work, the undocumented version people actually do, is where the biggest opportunities live. Then the four-question framework for mapping a workflow honestly.

0:45–1:15
30 min · work

Map your first workflow

10 min solo mapping using the four-question framework, 15 min in cross-unit pairs walking each other through, 5 min group share of what surprised them.

1:15–1:30
15 min · commit

Commit & close

Each person commits to finishing their workflow map before next session and identifying one pain point or AI opportunity. Logged in the Experiment Tracker. Close by previewing session 2.

The four-question framework

Question 1
What triggers it? event, request, escalation
Question 2
What are the actual steps? the real version, including workarounds
Question 3
Where does it break? delays, errors, frustration, rework
Question 4
What does "done" look like? clear handoff, finished artifact, decision
Post-session, within 24 hours

A recap goes out (from Bryton or Jazzy): 3–5 bullets, link to the Loop workspace, a reminder of the commitments made, and the date and time of session 2.

A watch-out on Loop

Loop's table features are weaker than Notion or Airtable. If the experiment tracker gets messy, move just that one piece to a simple SharePoint list or Excel in Teams; keep SOPs and curriculum in Loop.
